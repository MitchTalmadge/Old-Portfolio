HackTheU

Jan 2019 - Dec 2019 | Time Commitment: 150 Hours

HackTheU hosts free hackathons, workshops, and other events for college students all across Utah to promote our local tech community.

As Tech Directory of HackTheU, I led a team of web developers in a complete frontend overhaul, including a brand new responsive website served via CloudFlare CDN, a live IRC chat server for use during events, and a help queueing system; all hosted on Amazon AWS with the use of Docker. I also secured the organization's systems from attacks, including those stemming from often mis-configured email DNS settings. Lastly, I took key part in seeking out venues and organizing the logistics of said events.

CS Community Chatroom

2016 - Present | Time Commitment: Whenever I can, day or night.

Upon starting my college career at the University of Utah, I noticed a severe lack of communication between students of the School of Computing. For this reason, I started a chatroom exclusively for computer science students. I have spent countless hours of my life managing this chatroom with over 250 student members, helping students to understand concepts and assignments in any class I can, from early beginner classes to the most advanced.

To help me moderate the chatroom, I have heavily automated the chatroom through a custom bot that I developed using Java Spring Boot and the Discord APIs. Students, TAs, and Professors are automatically placed into roles and chatrooms based on their enrollment status.

FIRST LEGO League

2014 - Present | Time Commitment: 48 Hours (and counting)

Each year I support my local FIRST LEGO League chapter by helping to host the regional qualifier tournament. My role is to set up all the tech equipment, including audio, computers, tablets, servers, projectors, and power. I also control the music and act as the scorekeeper, inputting recorded scores from judges in real-time to be displayed on the scoreboards.
